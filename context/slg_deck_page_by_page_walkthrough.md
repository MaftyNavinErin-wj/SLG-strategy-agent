# SLG Deck Page-by-Page Walkthrough

Source deck: `sources/reference_decks/derrick_slg_mountain_20250923.pdf`

This note is a page-by-page walkthrough of the source deck. It is intentionally a distilled reading note, not a transcript or full extraction.

## How To Read The Deck

The deck is not generic SLG market research. It builds a strategy discussion frame:

1. Define why SLG is a category.
2. Split the category by structural mountains, not by theme.
3. Explain which products and teams changed the paradigm.
4. Trace growth sources across product experience, user entry, traffic, monetization, and team capability.
5. Convert the category read into coverage, research, and diligence questions.

The shortest thesis:

> SLG is a big-map war society, not a strategy skin. COK Like, Lvtu Like, ROK / fusion SLG, and casual COK Like differ because they organize resources, maps, combat, alliances, seasons, monetization, and traffic differently.

## Page 1 - Cover

The deck is titled `SLG 山头讨论`, dated September 2025.

This signals the purpose: build a "mountain" taxonomy and discussion material for SLG, rather than analyze one product or produce a generic market overview.

## Page 2 - Category Origin And Definition

This page is the foundation of the whole deck.

Core definition:

SLG mobile games use 4X elements, big-map conflict, multiplayer online strategy, and social organization. The core fun is strategy plus social interaction.

Historical sources:

1. PC 4X / grand strategy: `Civilization` contributes exploration, expansion, development, elimination, technology, economy, military, diplomacy, and multi-path victory.
2. Tactical strategy and historical simulation: `X-COM` and `Romance of the Three Kingdoms` contribute terrain, unit composition, generals, permanent loss, and the fantasy of unifying a country.
3. Web online strategy: `OGame`, `Travian`, `Kingdoms of Camelot`, and similar products contribute persistent online service, alliances, raiding, grudge-based social conflict, and long-term growth.
4. Mobile GaaS adaptation: `Game of War`, `Clash of Kings`, and `Lords Mobile` turn these ideas into mobile live-service, monetization, and UA-driven products.
5. Lvtu-like branch: `Kingdom Conquest`, `Sangokushi Conquest`, and `率土之滨` develop land-connection, card buildcraft, season reset, and territorial geopolitics.

Key read:

Do not understand SLG as a theme category. Its real category boundary is big-map PVP plus strategy-social systems.

## Page 3 - Main Mountain Split: COK Like vs. Lvtu Like

This is one of the deck's core pages.

COK Like:

1. Player pursuit: dominate the server.
2. Player identity: lord, alliance leader, whale, war driver.
3. Resource loop: city-building, resource acquisition, army growth, hero growth, speedups, and resources.
4. Map: coordinate-based big map, key buildings, resource points, teleport, and large-scale PVP.
5. Combat: mostly power, army amount, speed, troop counter, hero / skill strength, and rally execution.
6. Monetization: vertical growth, heroes / skills, speedups, and resources.
7. Social structure: looser alliance relationship, more dependent on big spenders and rally leaders.

Lvtu Like:

1. Player pursuit: build status and achievement inside the alliance.
2. Player identity: alliance member, officer, strategist, diplomat, executor.
3. Resource loop: land occupation, resource tiles, season progression, preserved paid assets, resettable seasonal assets.
4. Map: path / territory / land-connection structure, key passes, cities, and season battlefield.
5. Combat: pre-battle team composition, general / tactic pairing, season environment, report interpretation.
6. Monetization: card draw, horizontal combinations, season card pools, buildcraft depth.
7. Social structure: stronger discipline, alliance hierarchy, diplomacy, rewards / penalties, and cross-season relationships.

Key read:

COK Like is a personal-growth and whale-led war society. Lvtu Like is a season-geopolitical and alliance-discipline war society.

## Page 4 - Fusion SLG: Two Directions And Their Problems

This page discusses fusion attempts represented by `重返帝国` and `指尖无双`.

Fusion background:

From 2020 to 2024, many large-budget SLG projects chose a mix of ROK-style map / combat presentation and Lvtu-style horizontal card buildcraft / monetization.

Direction A: heavier fusion, represented by `重返帝国`.

1. Stronger real-time operation.
2. Higher art and map presentation.
3. More RTS-like map and combat expression.
4. Large team, large cost, long development cycle.
5. Problem: players need both strategy understanding and deep spending tolerance. Long-term experience can become too grindy and expensive.

Direction B: lighter fusion, represented by `指尖无双`.

1. Easier operation.
2. Simpler big-map PVP.
3. More social and idle-card filling.
4. Shorter development cycle and strong short-term performance.
5. Problem: long-term social relationship and economic robustness remain uncertain.

Key read:

Fusion SLG is not automatically a superior form. It can inherit the difficulty of both sides while weakening the social roles that make SLG durable.

## Page 5 - Global Market Size And China Share

This page shows the global SLG mobile market growing from roughly USD 47B in 2017 to USD 130B+ in 2025E.

The more important point is China developer share:

Chinese teams move from copying / adapting the category to defining its later paradigms, with market share rising toward about 90%.

Mountain structure:

1. COK Like remains the largest base.
2. Lvtu Like, fusion, and long-tail products contribute smaller but strategically important segments.
3. Casual COK Like drives the recent recovery and growth.

Key read:

The category's global growth is no longer mainly led by Western originators. Chinese R&D and publishing teams are defining the modern SLG playbook.

## Page 6 - Overseas SLG Development: Three Stages

The page frames overseas development in three periods.

2017-2020:

Classic COK Like is the mainstream. Success patterns become mature and somewhat fixed. Products include `Lords Mobile`, `State of Survival`, and `Rise of Kingdoms`.

2021-2023:

IDFA weakens classic payer-buying strategies. Teams begin validating casual sub-game entry and lighter front-end experiences.

2024-2025E:

Casual COK Like breaks out. `Last War: Survival`, `Whiteout Survival`, and `Kingshot` show that lower CPI entry can still convert into high-LTV SLG backend systems.

Key growth logic:

1. Casual gameplay lowers acquisition cost.
2. Ad platforms and attribution efficiency improve.
3. Broader users enter through simple loops.
4. SLG backend systems capture long-term LTV.

Key read:

Casual COK Like is not "SLG plus mini-game." It is a user-entry and traffic-efficiency shift.

## Page 7 - Overseas Company Landscape

This page maps major overseas-facing SLG products by peak annual revenue and stage.

2017-2020:

Large investment and M&A are common. Representative products include `Game of War`, `Mobile Strike`, `Lords Mobile`, `King of Avalon`, and `Clash of Kings`.

2021-2023:

The structure shifts toward producer entrepreneurship and publishers absorbing / supporting teams. Products include `State of Survival`, `Evony`, `Puzzles & Survival`, and `Top War`.

2024-2025E:

New breakout teams often inherit capability from earlier successful products. `Last War`, `Whiteout Survival`, and `Kingshot` become the new leaders.

Key read:

When doing coverage, look at founder and core-member lineage. Many winners are not generic new teams; they come from earlier SLG, casual, publishing, or UA success paths.

## Page 8 - Domestic SLG Development

Domestic SLG evolution differs from overseas because product iteration and quality upgrades are more visible.

2015-2018:

Classic COK Like and national-war products dominate. `率土之滨` becomes a category definer. `乱世王者` and similar products benefit from local theme / IP and strong distribution.

2019-2023:

`三国志战略版` expands the Lvtu-like audience through quality upgrade and simplification. `万国觉醒` validates ROK-like expression, though domestic durability is weaker than overseas. Fusion products emerge.

2024-2025E:

`三国谋定天下` continues simplifying Lvtu-like operation and compressing big-map PVP experience. Casual COK Like rises through mini-game platforms and UA infrastructure, represented by `无尽冬日` and `三国冰河时代`.

Platform factors:

Tencent distribution, Douyin growth, Bilibili user scale / spending power, and mini-game platform growth all affect the domestic landscape.

Key read:

Domestic SLG is shaped by product design, platform distribution, and traffic channel changes together.

## Page 10 - COK Like Revenue Structure Shift

This page documents the structural move from classic COK Like to casual COK Like.

Overseas:

Classic COK Like declines as a share of the COK-like market, while casual COK Like grows sharply in 2024-2025E.

A similar structural shift appears, with casual COK Like becoming a much larger share of the COK-like revenue pool.

This is not old COK simply recovering. The growth source changes because the user entry changes.

## Page 13 - COK Like Global Top-10 Concentration

The market is more fragmented. `Lords Mobile` and `King of Avalon` are important leaders, but many smaller teams can enter through reskins and low-cost UA.

## Page 15 - Lvtu Like And Fusion Product Composition

This page shows the evolution of Lvtu-like and fusion products.

Lvtu Like:

1. `率土之滨` grows quickly after 2015 through seasonal sandbox and hard-core GVG.
2. `三国志战略版` becomes the dominant product after 2019 and broadens the audience.
3. `三国谋定天下` grows after 2024, creating a more balanced structure among `率土`, `三战`, and `三谋`.

Fusion SLG:

Key read:

Lvtu Like has proven long-term category value. Fusion SLG still needs proof of durable economy, social organization, and repeatable product formula.

## Page 18 - Activity Trend: 率土之滨

This page shows `率土之滨` as a smaller but durable long-term product.

Important observations:

1. Activity is lower than `三战` / `三谋` but persistent.
2. Ten-year anniversary events, youth servers, limited seasons, and new battlefield modes create periodic waves.
3. The product remains the mechanism benchmark even if broader audience expansion is harder.

Key read:

`率土` is the category grammar. `三战` and `三谋` are broader-market translations of that grammar.

## Page 19 - Immersion, Realism, And Player Pursuit

This page starts the product-mechanics deep dive.

It explains SLG as a war simulation and a reflection of jungle society:

1. Limited resources create conflict.
2. PVE and city-building accumulate resources.
3. PVP validates growth.
4. Alliances provide protection and hierarchy.
5. GVG creates war, diplomacy, status, and honor.

Realism is not just art or theme. It comes from systems that make resource, territory, alliance, war, hierarchy, and honor feel credible.

Key read:

SLG sells social position. Players are not only chasing victory screens; they are chasing security, belonging, respect, and self-realization inside a war society.

## Page 20 - Entrance Theme / Gameplay Inspiration

This page explains where casual or new-entry SLG ideas come from.

Sources include:

1. PC single-player / indie inspirations: 4X, city-building, simulation, tower defense, survival, and social simulation.
2. Examples: `Frostpunk` to `Whiteout Survival`, `Thronefall` / tower-defense patterns, and survival / creature-management inspirations.
3. Hyper-casual / casual mobile inspirations: `Mob Control`, pin-pull, runner, merge, and short-session loops.
4. UA material validation: `Top War`, `Evony`, and `Last War` demonstrate that ad creative and front-end mechanics can test user appetite before deep system conversion.

Key read:

Entrance gameplay is valuable only if it can feed the SLG resource loop, growth loop, alliance loop, and monetization loop.

## Page 21 - Server / Season Loop And Reset Rules

This page compares reset rules across products such as WOS, ROK, `重返`, and `三谋`.

Rule of thumb:

1. Paid honor and directly paid assets tend to be preserved.
2. Grind, map control, alliance relation, ranking, and seasonal progress tend to reset.
3. Resettable components preserve balancing room and allow new season competition.

Examples of usually preserved assets:

1. Paid currency.
2. Heroes / generals.
3. Hero progression / general red-star progression.
4. Skills / tactics.
5. Equipment and mounts, depending on the product.

Examples of usually reset assets:

1. Alliance relationship.
2. Map control.
3. Territory, resource points, and central cities.
4. Migration items and seasonal rankings.

Key read:

Season reset is not wiping the account. It is a balance between asset preservation, competitive reset, and future commercial space.

## Page 22 - 三国谋定天下 Season Compression

This page explains how `三谋` compresses Lvtu-like season experience.

Important design moves:

1. The season is shorter and faster.
2. Birth-state neutrality reduces early over-fighting.
3. The neutrality mechanism can be removed later, increasing conflict.
4. Smaller map and faster sandbox movement compress the strategic arc.
5. Lower migration cost and faster relocation reduce travel friction.
6. Mid-season score bonuses create timing pressure.
7. Season warehouse / item copy systems allow limited preservation of seasonal choices.

Commercial loop:

Season card pools open with霸业 progress. Combat system, generals, tactics, season progress, alliance scores, city control, personal points, and paid resource rewards interlock.

Key read:

`三谋` lowers operation friction but does not necessarily lower total depth. It shifts complexity from early operation into season environment, buildcraft, and progression depth.

## Page 23 - COK Like Season / Cross-Server Loop

This page contrasts COK-like cycles with Lvtu-like cycles.

COK Like's major problem:

Numerical inflation and server ecology.

Common solution:

Cross-server matching, large operational events, event calendars, PVE chapters, PVP key-building conflict, and ranking rewards.

Example flow:

1. Selection / qualification: alliance or kingdom participates in events and qualifies for cross-server competition.
2. Matching and PVE: story choice, matching, exercises, world boss, limited tasks, item collection.
3. PVP and regional occupation: key buildings and revenge targets drive conflict.
4. Season / event settlement: ranked rewards cover many high-value players and distribute paid resources.

Key read:

COK Like uses operational events and cross-server matching to absorb inflation. Lvtu Like uses season reset as part of the gameplay core.

## Page 24 - Map Design And LOD

This page explains why map design matters.

Two meanings of map design:

1. It helps players read information and battlefield state.
2. It may create new strategic dimensions.

COK Like:

Coordinate-based map, point-and-surface relationship, often 3-4 LOD layers.

Lvtu Like:

Path / territory / land-connection map, point-line-surface relationship, often 5-7 LOD layers.

Fusion SLG:

Products like `重返帝国` and `战火勋章` use zoomable maps and map expression to affect combat strategy more directly, moving toward RTS-like decisions.

Key read:

Map is not just UI. It is where strategy becomes legible and where social war becomes spatial.

## Page 25 - New Strategy From Zoomable Maps

This page uses `万国觉醒` and `重返帝国` to show what zoomable / high-expression maps can add.

ROK examples:

1. Free city-building.
2. Free marching.
3. Terrain obstacles.
4. Army-vs-city siege expression.

`重返帝国` examples:

1. City interior LOD.
2. Three-dimensional siege.
3. Feints, flanking, and detours.
4. Forest ambush, river crossing, and chokepoints.

Key read:

Higher map expression is strategically valuable only when it changes decisions. Otherwise it is just expensive presentation.

## Page 26 - Lvtu-Like Three-Tier Resource Belt And 三国谋定天下 Simplification

This page describes the common Lvtu-like map structure:

1. Eight birth states.
2. Four resource states.
3. One central state.
4. Luoyang / central city as the ultimate objective.

`三谋` simplifies several operations:

1. Early-stage land pathing is reduced.
2. Auto-pathing and auto-land swap reduce player micro.
3. Players can move with the alliance more easily.
4. Migration cost and time are low.
5. Alliance siege camps and automatic siege reduce execution burden.

Key read:

`三谋` removes personal operation friction while preserving alliance-level movement and city-taking goals.

## Page 27 - Map Scenario Production And Innovation

This page says Lvtu-like and fusion SLG rely more heavily on map-mechanism updates.

Examples:

1. Weather / terrain strategy.
2. Frozen land changing land-connection rules.
3. Asymmetric maps and faction split.
4. Cultural / historical scenario themes.
5. Hard-core map mechanisms such as `八阵图`.

Production requirements:

1. 45-90 day season length for Lvtu-like.
2. Environment design, historical research, gameplay design, and UX implementation.
3. New generals / tactics matching the season environment.
4. Balance testing.

COK Like map updates are usually more about surface presentation, world bosses, key buildings, and atmosphere.

Key read:

For Lvtu Like, map scenario design is a real content-production and system-design capability test.

## Page 28 - Combat Strategy Stack

This page dissects combat, mainly through `率土之滨`.

Combat inputs include:

1. General draw and rarity.
2. General level.
3. Base and combat attributes.
4. Tactics / skills.
5. Troop count.
6. Stamina.
7. Rounds.
8. Troop type counters.
9. Command growth.
10. Attack range.
11. Skill type and effect.
12. Mounts, specialty troop types, buildings, titles, passive / command / active / pursuit sequence.

Core distinction:

COK Like often compresses strategy into a more vertical power comparison. Lvtu Like turns strategy into pre-battle composition, season adaptation, and report interpretation.

Key read:

Lvtu-like combat is a buildcraft and environment-understanding problem, not only a battle animation problem.

## Page 29 - Monetization: 三国谋定天下 Lowers Entry But Keeps Depth

This page explains `三谋` monetization design.

Entry-side simplification:

1. Lower draw price.
2. Higher orange-card probability.
3. Lower pity threshold.
4. More early free draws.
5. Third skill slot unlocks without consuming same-rarity cards.
6. Skill acquisition is easier and less tied to consuming purple cards.
7. No small card-pool trap for new cards.

Depth-side extension:

1. General red-star depth is increased.
2. Skill red-star progression adds value depth.
3. Graduation demand can be high even if initial team formation is cheaper.

Tradeoff of decoupling tactics from generals:

1. It gives designers more flexible tactic iteration.
2. It may increase player understanding difficulty.
3. It can accelerate meta churn.
4. It can reduce account asset stability if combinations retire quickly.

Key read:

`三谋` is not simply cheaper. It lowers early pain and increases accessibility, then preserves monetization depth through later progression.

## Page 30 - Social Structure

This page compares alliance structures across COK Like, Lvtu Like, and fusion-like products.

COK Like:

1. Alliance reason: big spender leadership and resource control.
2. Division of labor: rally leader and replaceable followers.
3. Strategic complexity: lower, often decided by strength, speed, and online headcount.
4. Stickiness: depends heavily on whale involvement; social pressure is lower.

Lvtu Like:

1. Alliance reason: season objective and settlement.
2. Division of labor: more diverse roles, including leadership, officers, strategists, diplomats, scouts, logistics, and executors.
3. Strategic complexity: high, including pathing, immunity timing, diplomacy, and coordinated warfare.
4. Stickiness: can create stable long-term communities; social pressure is higher.

Key read:

The key question is not alliance size. It is whether different alliance roles are meaningful and hard to replace.

## Page 31 - Capabilities Needed To Build SLG Social Relations

This page turns social structure into team capability requirements.

Required capabilities:

1. MMO / online-game engineering and operations.
2. Large-scale synchronized combat performance.
3. Cross-server matching.
4. Global-server and translation support for overseas products.
5. Anti-cheat, account clearing, and black-market control.
6. Migration, server merge, reserve-zone, and long-term server ecology rules.
7. Social system design that gives leaders, officers, diplomats, and active executors retention value.

The `重返帝国` warning:

Real-time combat can compress the pre-war planning space. If online headcount wins too often, managers and diplomats may feel less valuable, weakening the core social group that helps operate and retain the game.

Key read:

SLG social design requires full online-game operation experience. It is not solved by adding an alliance system.

## Page 32 - User, Region, And Traffic

This page connects SLG growth to traffic environment.

Regional read:

1. US and Western Europe are T1 overseas markets and contribute a large share.
2. Japan and the Middle East already have local-market competitors and theme fit.
3. Korea becomes an important incremental market for casual COK Like, with `Last War` and `Whiteout Survival` performing strongly.

Platform read:

1. Domestic Douyin, Bilibili, and mini-game platforms increase user reach and monetization.
2. IDFA makes classic paid-user buying harder.
3. AppLovin and similar ad platforms improve efficiency through long-tail traffic aggregation, attribution, hyper-casual data, and SLG data.

Key read:

SLG growth is highly correlated with advertising platform and attribution changes. Product innovation and traffic innovation must be read together.

## Page 33 - UA Capability And Coverage Questions

This final page converts the deck into action.

UA infrastructure includes:

1. Pre-launch campaign setup and account allocation.
2. In-flight monitoring and optimization.
3. Post-campaign review.
4. Creative asset management.
5. Attribution and data engineering.
6. High-frequency product-publishing feedback.

Important principles:

1. Creative is part of the player experience flow, not just marketing decoration.
2. Low-quality fake ads may create platform risk.
3. Marketing Tech is real technology and can be a durable advantage.
4. Capital matters because ad platforms will accept competing spend from similar products.

Coverage directions:

1. Targeted attack on lost users in a known subgenre.
2. Country takeover, such as increasing Korea localization for `Last War`.
3. Retaining non-GVG users through optional content.
4. Multi-platform strategy: mini-game / web for simple casual COK Like, PC / emulator for ROK and Lvtu-like operation-heavy products.

Key read:

The teams worth tracking are those that combine product design, SLG backend understanding, UA infrastructure, and platform-specific distribution execution.

## Strategic Takeaways

1. SLG is a big-map war society. It is not just strategy combat or theme packaging.
2. COK Like solves personal growth, whale leadership, coordinate-map war, and cross-server event loops.
3. Lvtu Like solves alliance discipline, land geopolitics, season objectives, and horizontal buildcraft.
4. ROK / fusion SLG tries to upgrade map and combat expression, but can damage the social roles that make SLG durable.
5. Casual COK Like is the recent growth center because it changes user entry and traffic efficiency while preserving high-LTV SLG backend systems.
6. Future product judgment should ask whether growth comes from experience innovation, user expansion, traffic change, monetization change, or team capability migration.
7. Future team judgment should focus on system design, social operation, season production, UA infrastructure, data engineering, and long-term live operations.

## Practical Coverage Questions

For COK Like / casual COK Like:

1. What is the low-friction entry loop?
2. How does it feed the resource and alliance loop?
3. How much non-GVG content exists for broader users?
4. What is the team's UA infrastructure and creative testing capability?
5. Which region or platform is the likely breakthrough wedge?

For Lvtu Like:

1. What operation friction is reduced?
2. What strategic depth is preserved or moved later?
3. How strong is season map / scenario production?
4. Can the product migrate alliances and guild leaders?
5. Does monetization lower entry pain while preserving depth?

For fusion SLG:

1. Does real-time map / combat expression create new strategic decisions?
2. Does it preserve roles for leaders, officers, diplomats, and planners?
3. Is the long-term economy robust enough?
4. Is user understanding cost acceptable?
5. Does the team have both MMO engineering and SLG social-operation capability?
