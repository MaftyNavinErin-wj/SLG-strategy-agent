# SLG Output Hypotheses Benchmarked Against The Derrick Deck

Status: directional hypotheses, not measured benchmarks

Last updated: 2026-07-23

Primary source: `sources/reference_decks/derrick_slg_mountain_20250923.pdf`

## Purpose

The core taxonomy defines product structure. This note tracks what that structure is expected to produce in activity, monetization, payer mix, retention, and server ecology.

The causal stack is:

> A x G x R core structure, implemented through supporting features -> vertical / horizontal power and monetization regime -> live-ops optimization -> monitored outputs.

Higher-tier structure sets the characteristic direction, ceiling, and failure modes. Lower-tier execution determines where the product lands inside that structural envelope.

## Confidence Rule

1. High confidence: statements directly supported by the deck's mechanism comparison or multiple numeric pages.
2. Medium confidence: directional output inference supported by activity or market proxies.
3. Low confidence: payer rate, ARPPU, segment retention, and LTV claims without direct cohort or transaction data.

## Activity And Retention Read

The deck's pages 16-18 support curve-shape judgments more than exact retention judgments.

1. `三国谋定天下` shows repeated season-opening playtime spikes followed by decline inside each season, while its active base falls from the launch peak.
2. `三国志战略版` shows a more mature, gradually declining active base, with operational events and new modes producing temporary playtime lifts.
3. `率土之滨` shows a smaller but relatively stable mature core, with season and anniversary events producing limited lifts.

Working interpretation:

1. Lutu-like retention is highly conditional on alliance integration and season renewal.
2. The strongest surviving cohorts can have high cross-season social retention even while the total active base declines.
3. Season spikes do not by themselves prove high player retention; they can reflect recurring reactivation of a shrinking core.
4. Group retention and group churn must be measured together because an alliance leader or officer cohort can move many users at once.

## Commercialization Read

The deck supports three important corrections.

1. Lower entry cost is not equivalent to lower ARPU. `三国谋定天下` combines cheaper acquisition with deeper red and skill graduation.
2. Casual entry is not equivalent to shallow monetization. Casual COK has demonstrated revenue efficiency at least comparable to its user share.
3. Fusion monetization engines can add, but retention engines do not automatically add. Selling both seasonal roster renewal and war throughput can produce high theoretical ARPU while reducing willingness to remain or fight.

## What The Deck Does Not Yet Prove

The following remain hypotheses until transaction and cohort data are available:

1. Actual payer conversion by mountain.
2. ARPPU and revenue concentration by payer percentile.
3. Mid-spender retention relative to whales and non-payers.
4. Revenue per active-war day and revenue after the season result becomes predictable.
5. Cross-season retention conditional on alliance survival.
6. Officer churn multipliers and group-correlated churn.
7. Whether horizontal monetization produces a consistently thicker payer middle than vertical monetization.
8. Whether fusion products underperform because of structural double pressure or product-specific IP, balance, UA, and execution.

## Metrics For A Future Data Pass

1. Effective war-active days and date when outcome probability crosses a decisive threshold.
2. DAU, playtime, payer conversion, ARPU, and revenue by season day.
3. Payer distribution: top 0.1%, 1%, 10%, mid-spender, and low-spender contribution.
4. Spend split between acquisition breadth, vertical depth, recovery throughput, and social / status goods.
5. S1-to-S2 and S2-to-S3 retention by alliance outcome and role.
6. Alliance survival, migration, officer churn, and correlated member churn.
7. Match-strength delta, avoidance behavior, sandbagging, and heat / reward farming.
8. Old-asset use rate and relative utility after each season transition.
