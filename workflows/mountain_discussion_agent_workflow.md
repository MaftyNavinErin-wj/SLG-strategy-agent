# Mountain Discussion Agent Workflow

Use this workflow to produce strategy materials at the same granularity as the SLG mountain deck.

## Mission

Build category strategy material that can support:

1. Internal strategy discussion.
2. BD / KA coverage priorities.
3. Investment, partnership, publishing, or ecosystem theses.
4. Product and team capability judgment.

The output should not be generic industry research. It should explain the structure of a category and where future action should point.

## Standard Analysis Flow

### 1. Category Definition

Answer:

1. What is the core player fantasy?
2. What is the core loop?
3. What makes this category distinct from adjacent categories?
4. What is the long-term pursuit that sustains players?

### 2. Historical Sources

Trace:

1. PC / console origins.
2. Web / online adaptation.
3. Mobile / F2P / live service adaptation.
4. Platform and traffic shifts.
5. Key products that changed the formula.

### 3. Mountain Split

Do not split by theme first. Split by structural differences:

1. Core loop.
2. Map / level / world structure.
3. Combat / interaction model.
4. Progression and economy.
5. Social structure.
6. Monetization model.
7. User entry / traffic model.

### 4. Market Structure

Analyze:

1. Global and domestic size.
2. Revenue and user share by mountain.
3. Head concentration.
4. Growth and decline sources.
5. Regional differences.
6. Platform shifts.

### 5. Product Evolution

For each stage:

1. Which product defined the paradigm?
2. Which product expanded the audience?
3. Which product improved economics?
4. Which product failed despite resources?
5. What did the winning team understand that others missed?

### 6. Product Mechanics Deep Dive

Use category-specific layers. For SLG, use:

1. Immersion / substitution.
2. Server / season loop.
3. Map design.
4. Combat strategy.
5. Social structure.
6. User acquisition / traffic.

For a new category, create an equivalent teardown stack.

### 7. User And Traffic

Analyze:

1. Core users vs. new users.
2. Entry fantasy.
3. Platform / channel fit.
4. UA creative.
5. Community and content ecosystem.
6. Retention of non-core users.

### 8. Team Capability

Infer what kind of team can win:

1. Prior successful project experience.
2. Design taste and category literacy.
3. Technical and pipeline capability.
4. Live ops / social operations.
5. Data and UA infrastructure.
6. Ability to migrate from adjacent genres.

### 9. Future Opportunities

Separate:

1. Short-term commercial certainty.
2. Medium-term product innovation.
3. Long-term category redefinition.
4. Coverage targets.
5. Open questions needing validation.

## Inputs Needed

Minimum information package:

1. Product list with launch date, developer, publisher, region, platform.
2. Revenue / download / DAU / MAU / user trend data.
3. Gameplay recording and personal play notes.
4. Monetization and progression screenshots.
5. Community sentiment from player forums and creator content.
6. UA creative examples.
7. Team background and organization history.
8. Platform / policy / traffic environment changes.

## Output Standard

A good mountain discussion material should include:

1. A one-page thesis.
2. A category evolution map.
3. A mountain taxonomy table.
4. Market structure charts.
5. Product comparison tables.
6. Core system teardown pages.
7. Team capability assessment.
8. Coverage implications.
9. Open questions and next research plan.

## Quality Bar

Every conclusion should answer:

1. What changed?
2. Why did it matter to players?
3. Why did it matter to business results?
4. Which team was able to execute?
5. What should we do next?
